# 06 — Red-Team Review of the Converged Architecture

**Method.** After the debate converged (`03_debate_log.md`, 6 rounds) and the final spec was written (`05_final_architecture.md`), three **independent adversarial reviewers** — none of whom participated in the debate — were tasked to *break* the converged design, each from a different lens: an **operator** (real SOC deployment), an **implementation engineer** (LangGraph buildability), and an **evidence/reasoning skeptic** (citation integrity + convergence blind spots). Convergence is exactly where shared blind spots hide; this review is the antidote.

**Headline verdict.** The design's *backbone* (deterministic per-task graphs D1/D3, the two-store read/write split) is genuinely sound and buildable. But everything load-bearing in the *synthesis* — the cross-task dispatcher, the tiering control signal, the promotion loop, the confidence calculus, the human gate's throughput and efficacy — is specified at the level of **intent, not interface**, and contains **two flat internal contradictions** plus an **unexamined simpler alternative** that may be the actually-most-feasible answer. The citations are real and correctly attributed (a strong result); the *reasoning built on them* over-reaches its evidence in several places. Net: trust the facts; trust the converged architecture **considerably less than its confident framing invites** until the items below are resolved.

---

## A. Convergent findings — flagged independently by ≥2 reviewers (highest confidence)

These are the strongest signals: different lenses, same flaw, arrived at independently.

### C1 — The promotion loop poisons the "un-poisonable" KG *(operator F5 + implementation F5)* — **CRITICAL, architecture-changing**
The spec's central safety pillar is an "immutable, read-only, **no agent write path**, structurally un-poisonable" grounding KG (§2.2). But the extensibility story (§2.3, §3) says "validated novel pivots are **promoted into new KG edges**." These cannot both hold: promotion *is* an agent-adjacent write path into the grounding store, laundered through a single human approval — exactly the approval the spec admits (caveat 2) is fallible against on-graph misdirection. An attacker who lands **one** on-graph-misdirection approval gets a malicious edge promoted to grounding truth for **all future hunts**, no longer flagged low-confidence. The "structurally un-poisonable" claim is **false** as written.
**Fix:** a third, **versioned, human-curated "promoted-edges" overlay** store, read by Tier-1 as grounding but tagged `provenance=promoted` and confidence-capped; promotion gated by a separate *offline* curation step (multi-sign-off / quarantine), **not** the runtime `interrupt()`. Stop calling the composite "immutable / no-write-path."

### C2 — "Immutable" KG vs. ATT&CK shipping ~2×/year breaks deterministic replay *(operator F8 + implementation F6)* — **High, architecture-changing**
"Immutable" conflates two meanings: "no agent writes it at runtime" (a real safety property) and "it never changes" (false — ATT&CK re-releases ~2×/yr; CVEs land daily). The technique-ID-resolution invariant hard-fails on unresolved IDs, so an upstream release is a fleet-wide break; and the audit schema carries `run_id` + provenance but **no `kg_version`**, so deterministic replay (the stated reason for choosing deterministic control flow over swarm) silently breaks on the next ATT&CK bump.
**Fix:** make the KG **versioned + content-addressed**; pin every run to a `kg_version` snapshot in the trace/artifact schema; define a deprecation-migration path for stored artifacts. Add to Open Problems.

### C3 — The tiering control signal ("genuine no-KG-edge miss") is undefined, making the miss-rate instrumentation circular *(implementation F4/F10 + evidence F3)* — **Critical to the tiering logic**
Tier-1→Tier-2 escalation fires on a "genuine no-edge miss," and the default tier is "set by the instrumented miss-rate." But KG retrieval over a connected CVE→CWE→CAPEC→ATT&CK graph **almost never returns the empty set** — the real signal is "nothing scored above a relevance threshold τ," i.e., the fuzzy re-ranker judgment the spec elsewhere calls deterministic. So: "miss" is a *threshold artifact*, the "miss is a high-value detection signal" claim collapses (most misses are tuning artifacts at high τ), and the instrumentation is **circular** (it measures the consequence of the τ knob it's meant to inform). Worse, the design is framed to "win" under *any* outcome — if the miss-rate is high it "degrades gracefully to reasoner-heavy," which is just *collapsing into Position B*, the position it claimed to refine away. That makes parts of the convergence **unfalsifiable**.
**Fix:** define miss = `max re-ranker score over k-hop frontier < τ`; make §5-A a **τ-sweep**, not a single number; state the falsifiable prediction (*if* miss-rate is high at sane τ, the converged design is wrong and B was right) and a real promotion *decision rule* with a human gate.

### C4 — The confidence-floor gate is both unbuildable (no calculus) and self-defeating (suppresses the true-novel, passes the plausible-false) *(operator F4 + implementation F9)* — **High, architecture-changing**
"Default-deny below a confidence floor" needs one comparable scalar per proposal, but confidence comes from **non-composable** sources: AURA's ~63% top-1 is a *model-level* stat (not per-instance), re-ranker scores are *relevance* not *correctness*, sign-off is *binary*. There is no defined aggregation, so the floor is unbuildable as specified. And it's **adversarially exploitable + self-contradicting**: a well-grounded on-graph decoy scores *high* (passes), while the rare genuine KG-miss — which §2.3 itself calls "the most interesting observation a hunt can produce" — scores *low* and is **silently denied before it can escalate**. The default-deny floor and the "always surface a genuine miss" principle directly contradict.
**Fix:** replace the scalar floor with a **typed confidence record** + a *rule* (`deny if attribution-class-ceiling < floor OR re-rank < τ OR critic failed`); **never silently deny** — a genuine KG-miss escalates regardless of confidence; reserve default-deny for high-volume, low-novelty, on-graph proposals only; calibrate confidence (reliability diagram) before any floor is set.

### C5 — The per-pipeline critics are the highest-risk component, yet gate from day one with unmeasured error rates and no independence mechanism *(operator F9 + implementation F8)* — **High**
Critics sit *before* the human as the verification layer (MAST-justified). For D1 (set-coverage) the critic is genuinely deterministic. But D3 (refinement-validity) and D4 (pivot-consistency) are **semantic** judgments that, absent ground truth, are made by an LLM grounded against the *same* KG the proposer used — i.e., correlated errors, the self-critique paradox the design claims to dodge. The spec mandates measuring false-confirmation rate but provides **no independence mechanism** and **no release-gate** tying that measurement to deployment.
**Fix:** split **deterministic structural critics** (D1 coverage; D2 precondition = graph reachability check) from **semantic critics** (D3/D4); for semantic critics mandate independence levers (different model / different grounding view / reduce to constraint-satisfaction); make per-critic false-confirmation-rate a **per-pipeline release gate** (shadow-mode until cleared).

### C6 — The human gate's *efficacy* is an unproven hypothesis, yet the whole safety case rests on it *(operator F1/F2 + evidence F1)* — **High**
The Cyber Defense Benchmark (no model passes hunting) justifies *both* "no autonomous disposer" *and* the gate's safety — but it is **silent on whether a human catches a fluent, well-grounded-but-wrong proposal**. The spec itself admits (caveat 3) "whether evidence-first framing reduces rubber-stamping is empirical and unproven." So the safety case leans entirely on a mechanism marked *unvalidated*, and "surface evidence not verdicts" may *increase* automation bias by lending false thoroughness (the analyst must re-derive the verdict under time pressure, hundreds of times). The "4% sought verdicts" stat is from *voluntary, low-stakes* LLM use and is being mis-applied to *mandatory, high-stakes* gating.
**Fix:** state plainly that HITL is currently **unfalsified, not validated**; pre-register a **kill-criterion** (if test B shows evidence-first does not beat verdict-framing and on-graph false-approval > X%, *lower* autonomy rather than hold it); instrument analyst **dwell-time per gate** in production as the rubber-stamp tell.

## B. Additional critical / high findings (single-lens, but serious)

- **B1 — The cross-task dispatcher's composition topology is never specified** *(impl F1/F12)* — **CRITICAL build blocker.** "Independent graphs" AND "one dispatcher they hand pivots to and resume from" are in tension, and "emit a pivot-request event" is **not a LangGraph primitive**. Without choosing (parent-graph-with-subgraphs vs. separate runtimes + external broker) and defining the `PivotRequest` type, the concurrent-pivot reducer, and `correlation_id`-keyed resume routing, Stage 3 cannot be built. *Fix:* make the dispatcher a **parent `StateGraph`**, pipelines = subgraphs invoked via `Command(goto)`/`Send`; redefine "independent" as "independently-authored modules"; specify the resume contract.

- **B2 — The uniform human gate is a throughput collapse on D5's iterative loop** *(op F1)* — **CRITICAL operational.** Gating *every* externally-executed query conflates "reading your own SIEM" with "acting on the outside world." A day-long hunt generates dozens–hundreds of gate stops on the most contended resource in the building. *Fix:* gate only **side-effecting / external-acting** steps; read-only queries against already-authorized telemetry do **not** interrupt; batch-and-checkpoint disposition at natural decision boundaries; define a queue-overflow / shift-change policy.

- **B3 — The simpler D1+D3-only MVP was never argued against — it was excluded by the motion's framing** *(evidence F6)* — **High; challenges the recommendation.** The only two *deployment-proven* tasks are D1 (ASTRIDE/STRIDE-GPT) and D3 (DefenseWeaver). The entire dispatcher/coupling/Tier-2 apparatus exists to serve D4 pivoting and D5 hunting — D5 is at the autonomy floor (no model passes) and D4 rests on cardinality-fragile grounding (see B5). The convergence **built the speculative ~70% and labeled the proven ~30% the "backbone."** A reviewer optimizing for the literal motion ("most feasible *today*") could argue the honest answer is **ship D1+D3, stop, and defer the rest behind instrumentation** — and that was never argued down. *Fix:* the spec must explicitly compare against a D1+D3-only MVP and justify why the coupling machinery earns its risk *now* rather than later.

- **B4 — The "deterministic / cheap" Tier-1 re-ranker is sourced to an explicitly *LLM-based* re-ranker** *(impl F3)* — **High.** The Cost-12 and Auditability-8 wins rest on Tier-1 being "deterministic and replayable," but it cites TechniqueRAG's *LLM* re-ranker. An LLM re-ranker is neither deterministic nor free. *Fix:* use a **non-LLM deterministic re-ranker** (cross-encoder / learned-to-rank with frozen weights), or downgrade the claim to "replayable given pinned model + cached I/O" and re-state the cost.

- **B5 — "Grounds" ≠ "grounds correctly"; ordinary retrieval error reproduces the adversarial blind spot** *(evidence F2)* — **High.** The technique-ID invariant only checks an ID resolves to *some* retrieved object, not the *right* one. At ~600-technique cardinality (the very reason TechniqueRAG / Hierarchical-RAG exist) with re-ranker accuracy plausibly ~60–70%, routine **mis-grounding** to a plausible-but-wrong technique is indistinguishable to the off-graph detector from a correct one — the same hole as on-graph misdirection, but from ordinary error, not an adversary. *Fix:* measure re-ranker *accuracy* (not just resolution success); acknowledge mis-grounding as a first-class failure mode.

- **B6 — The off-graph detector guards against *accidents*, not *adversaries*** *(evidence F8)* — **Medium-High.** A rational injection attacker steers toward a *real, on-graph, citable* decoy (the admitted blind spot) — the easy case for the attacker — not an off-graph fabrication. The detector catches the attack nobody would bother with. *Fix:* reframe the off-graph detector as a **hallucination guard**, not an injection defense; the injection story is honestly covered only by the (unproven) human gate + canaries.

- **B7 — No SIEM / EDR / SOAR / ticketing integration story** *(op F6)* — **High adoption risk.** The spec is entirely LangGraph-internal; not one word on Splunk/Sentinel/CrowdStrike/XSOAR/ServiceNow. Gates in a bespoke interrupt-UI that analysts must watch *in addition to* their console will be routed around within a week. *Fix:* deliver gates into the **existing** approval queue, query telemetry via **existing** SIEM/EDR APIs under existing RBAC, emit outputs the existing tools consume — a **Stage-0** requirement, not a Stage-5 nicety.

- **B8 — Misdirection defenses (canaries, N-parallel hypotheses, analyst rotation) are costed at zero and contradict the cost claims** *(op F3)* — **High.** N parallel independent hypotheses multiplies the $18/run baseline *and* the gate load N×; analyst rotation presumes a pool of interchangeable senior hunters most SOCs don't have and destroys hunt continuity; injecting canaries into production telemetry risks polluting detections, firing real SOAR, and audit/compliance problems. *Fix:* cost them explicitly with staffing assumptions, route canaries through a **shadow channel** (never production feeds), or honestly downgrade the claim to "bounded blast radius + post-hoc audit."

## C. Medium / lower findings (worth fixing, not blocking)

- **`interrupt()` idempotency vs. per-claim audit logging are in direct tension** *(impl F7)* — appending a trace node *before* `interrupt()` double-logs on resume; side effects + their trace must go strictly *after* the interrupt, with an idempotency key per external query. Day-long durability needs a named external durable-execution layer (Temporal/Restate), not "validate in Stage 4."
- **Recursive `Send` fan-out (D3) + per-expansion critic blows the LangGraph `recursion_limit`** *(impl F2)* — the spec conflates *domain tree depth* with *super-step count*; multiple simultaneous interrupts need batching + a resume-map keyed by interrupt ID.
- **CyberLLM-FINDS is cited selectively** *(evidence F5)* — KG+GNN scored 8.00 vs RAG 7.87 (a 0.13 margin on ~8), but RAG actually won **3 of 5** judged categories; the doc cites only the category KG won. Cite neutrally or drop.
- **The 2509.07939 magnitude (86–206% fewer resources) is an offense-side (pentest) proxy over-transferred to defensive tasks** *(evidence F4)* — the *directional* claim survives; the *magnitude* should not be repeated as if it transfers.
- **Day-2 ops is entirely unowned** *(op F5)* — KG ingestion/versioning, 5 critic monitors, floor tuning, injection-rule updates, artifact-store curation: add an explicit RACI.
- **Stage-1 "minimal audit schema" → retrofit churn** *(impl F13)* — define the final audit schema first, or label Stage-1 traces dev-only.
- **Test B (on-graph false-approval) needs a planted-decoy oracle** *(impl F11)* — buildable only as a **seeded-decoy lab benchmark** (synthetic ground truth), an explicit lower-bound proxy; production exposure is unmeasurable without an oracle.

## D. Citation integrity — the good news

The evidence-skeptic attempted to falsify every load-bearing source, including the suspicious 2026-dated arXiv IDs and the oddly-specific numbers. **All verified real and roughly correctly applied:** MAST (2503.13657), self-critique paradox (2310.01798), Cyber Defense Benchmark (2604.19533; $17.98/run is specifically Opus 4.6, rounded to ~$18), SOC field study (2508.18947), AURA (2506.10175; 63.33/73.33 exact), DefenseWeaver (2504.18083), CORTEX (2510.00311), Policy-Guided (2603.23966), TechniqueRAG (2505.11988), Hierarchical ATT&CK RAG (2604.14166), ASTRIDE (2512.04785), LangChain multi-agent benchmark, OWASP LLM01. **No fabricated citations.** The flaws are in *reasoning over* the evidence (B3–B6, C3, C6), not the evidence itself.

## E. Disposition — what changes the architecture vs. what's a documented caveat

**Must change the architecture (contradictions / blockers):** C1 (promotion-loop poisoning → overlay store), C3 (define "miss" / falsifiable), C4 (confidence record + never-silently-deny), B1 (dispatcher topology + resume contract), B2 (gate only side-effecting steps).
**Must change claims / add specification:** C2 (versioned KG + `kg_version` in trace), C5 (split + release-gate critics), B4 (deterministic re-ranker or downgraded claim), B5/B6 (mis-grounding as failure mode; off-graph detector reframed as hallucination guard), C6 (HITL = unfalsified-not-validated + kill-criterion).
**Must be added as design/ops requirements:** B7 (integration as Stage-0), B8 (cost the misdirection defenses honestly / shadow canaries), day-2 RACI, durable-execution dependency.
**Honest re-frame of the recommendation:** B3 — explicitly evaluate a **D1+D3-only MVP** and justify (or defer) the coupling machinery; the proven core should be shipped and the speculative synthesis instrumented-first, not presented as equally settled.

**Bottom line.** The debate produced a *rich, evidence-honest narrative* and a sound Stage-1/Stage-2 core, but the synthesis's center of gravity (the dispatcher, tiering, promotion, confidence, and gate) is a **design sketch wearing a decision record's clothes**, with two flat internal contradictions and a complexity ratchet the rubric's own weights (Feasibility 25 + Cost 12, held by Position A throughout) did not justify. Remediation in `05_final_architecture.md` Rev 3.
